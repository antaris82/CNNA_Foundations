## Interpretation

The candidate adjoint formula is well-defined when the local DtN role-plane pairing is positive and reasonably conditioned.  This by itself is not the nontrivial gate; it is mostly linear algebra.

The nontrivial closure question is whether the adjoints of the generated operators remain in the operator system visibly generated by growth.  A positive result would require small reverse-span residuals for T* and for the mixed defect C*.  Large residuals mean that the current L/T operator system is not yet closed under the candidate adjoint, even though the DtN energy pairing itself defines a formal adjoint.

Next gate after positive closure would be a real *-operator-system test.  If closure is weak, the next step is to enrich the generated operator set, probably by including port-specific rank-one handoff maps or record/live separated blocks, before attempting J.
